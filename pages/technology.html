Open source, peer reviewed, professionally audited, and easy to use encryption for everyone.

Making security accessible.

The vast majority of communications sent today are unencrypted and vulnerable to prying eyes and data thieves. Until now, encryption tools have been notoriously difficult to use and were reserved for programmers and software engineers. Peerio was designed for everyone. After all, what good are encrypted messages with no one to send them to? Whether you're a tech novice or computer guru, Peerio was designed for you.

End-to-end encryption. What it means. Why it matters.

End-to-end encryption means that your messages and files are encrypted in every step, not just on the way to a server. When data is encrypted end-to-end, only the sender and the intended recipients can access it. Meanwhile, the service providers themselves can’t access your data, even if they wanted to.

With Peerio, when you write a message and attach a file, your computer encrypts, or “locks”, your message with your private key before sending it over the network. It may be on our network, but it’s still locked, even from us. When your contact receives your message, only they will be capable of decrypting, or ‘unlocking’ your message or file.

With most other services, data is encrypted only when being transmitted to their servers. After that, your messages and files are stored on their servers without encryption. You’re trusting that they will protect your data, that they may keep for months, or even years. Some of the biggest networks in the world have had serious leaks of personal information, passwords, communications records, and credit card and banking info. Some examples include: Apple, Adobe, eBay, and Sony.

Open source and peer reviewed.

Making secure encryption systems isn’t easy. That’s why Peerio’s end-to-end encryption technology is open source and peer-reviewed. We use state-of-the-art encryption methods, and anyone in the world can verify that they work the way they’re supposed to—even you. You can review Peerio's code at GitHub.
