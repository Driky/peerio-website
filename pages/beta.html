Peerio is in Beta

Why release software in beta?

Great development teams and programmers can design great software, but it's users who will make it perfect. You know better than anyone what you want from your messaging and cloud storage service. As Peerio continues to develop and innovate new features, mobile support, and more, your thoughts, your ideas, and your opinions are shared directly with our developers to ensure Peerio is designed for you, by you.

Secure from the start.

Peerio's security builds off miniLock, an open-source, peer reviewed, and proven encryption standard developed by Peerio's lead software engineer and security expert. Our development team is also responsible for CryptoCat, one of the only privacy tools to receive a perfect score from the Electronic Frontier Foundation's secure messaging scorecard (make “secure messaging scorecard” a link to https://www.eff.org/secure-messaging-scorecard). Peerio meets and surpasses the standards of "military-grade" encryption, and holds itself to the most advanced encryption protocols of contemporary cryptography. All of Peerio’s code, including its implementation of curve25519 elliptic curve cryptography and a secure server, has been audited by experts at the forefront of cryptography research and system penetration testing. Technology evolves quickly, and that's why Peerio undergoes third-party security audits every six months to ensure that our technology is secured against new threats and backed by industry-leading experts and researchers. As Peerio's features and services continue to evolve and grow, you can rest assured that your data and privacy will be protected every step of the way.

A collaborative project.

When you trust a tool to protect you, you should be able to know how it works. Peerio's code is open-source and publicly available at GitHub(link to our GitHub) for you to review and comment on. We encourage ongoing peer review, security research, and vulnerability testing, and hope you'll join our community of researchers and contributors. Peerio offers a bug bounty(link to bug bounty page, or document) for anyone able to identify a serious security vulnerability in Peerio. Privacy is a universal concern, but strong encryption is only one part of a solution. For encryption to offer security and privacy to individuals around the world, it needs to be easy and intuitive for people to use and enjoy. We want to know what you think about online security, the tools you use or don't use because of privacy concerns, and what Peerio can do to enhance your ability to maintain privacy online. Share your thoughts with us at feedback@peerio.com.
